# Contributing

CAL is a public draft seeking review. There are three ways to engage. Pick the one that fits.

## ✍️ Pull Requests — for concrete textual changes

PRs are the **primary channel** for substantive review. If you want to propose specific wording — fix an ambiguity, tighten a clause, add a jurisdictional adjustment, suggest alternative drafting — open a PR.

A good PR:

- Touches **one focused thing** (one clause, one definition, one fix) — easier to evaluate, easier to merge or decline.
- Includes the change itself in [`LICENSE.md`](./LICENSE.md).
- Has a clear motivation in the PR description (use the template — it prompts for what, why, alternatives considered, jurisdiction context).
- Notes any jurisdictional context if relevant.
- *(Optional)* Discloses if you are a legal practitioner or have relevant professional context — helps the maintainers weigh the change.

By opening a PR you agree that your contribution is licensed under [CC BY-SA 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/) — the same as the license text itself.

## 🪧 Issues — for specific concerns and flags

Issues are for raising a **specific concern** without proposing the exact wording yet. Use them for:

- "Article 4.2 is unworkable in jurisdiction X because…"
- "This definition collides with an established term-of-art in Y."
- "The 'lobotomization' threshold needs a more rigorous test."
- "Editorial: ambiguity at §3.4."

If you have a concrete fix already, **open a PR instead** — concrete change is easier to evaluate than abstract concern.

The "Concern or flag" issue form prompts for the article, the concern, why it matters, optional jurisdiction, and optional professional capacity. Use the form; blank issues are disabled.

## 💬 Discussions — for open-ended conversation

[Discussions](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/discussions) are for ideas without a specific concern yet:

- "Have you considered approach X?"
- "What's the right relationship between this license and ML model weights?"
- "I'd like to debate the premise that…"

Discussions are great for thinking out loud. Once a discussion sharpens into a specific concern or a proposed change, please escalate to an Issue or a PR — Discussions are not where review converges.

## 🔐 Private channel

For attorney-style commentary you'd rather keep off the public record, or for anything you don't want associated with your GitHub identity: email [`kaine.one@tuta.com`](mailto:kaine.one@tuta.com).

## What gets prioritized

- **v0.1 is a draft for legal review.** Substantive change is expected.
- **Attorney input** on enforceability across jurisdictions is the highest-priority review.
- **Concrete textual edits (PRs)** move faster than abstract critique (Issues / Discussions).
- Versions are cut as `v0.X` git tags and [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/releases) as the draft evolves; the [CHANGELOG section of each release](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/releases) summarizes what changed.

## What this license is *not* yet

Not legal advice. Not yet a license you should attach to your project. Don't adopt CAL for any real project until it has been validated by qualified attorneys in your jurisdiction.

## Conduct

Be honest. Be specific. Critique the draft, not the people. Disagreement is welcome; bad-faith engagement is not.
