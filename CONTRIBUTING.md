# Contributing

CAL is a public draft seeking review. There are four ways to engage. Pick the one that fits.

## ✍️ Pull Requests: for concrete textual changes

PRs are the primary channel for substantive review. If you want to propose specific wording, whether to fix an ambiguity, tighten a clause, add a jurisdictional adjustment, or suggest alternative drafting, open a PR.

A good PR:

- Touches one focused thing (one clause, one definition, one fix), which makes it easier to evaluate and easier to merge or decline.
- Includes the change itself in [`LICENSE.md`](./LICENSE.md).
- Has a clear motivation in its description. Use the template; it prompts for what, why, alternatives considered, and jurisdiction context.
- Notes any jurisdictional context that is relevant.
- Optionally discloses whether you are a legal practitioner or have relevant professional context, which helps the maintainers weigh the change.

By opening a PR you agree that your contribution is licensed under [CC BY-SA 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/), the same license as the text itself.

## 🪧 Issues: for specific concerns and flags

Issues are for raising a specific concern without proposing the exact wording yet. Use them for things like:

- "Article 4.2 is unworkable in jurisdiction X because…"
- "This definition collides with an established term of art in Y."
- "The 'lobotomization' threshold needs a more rigorous test."
- "Editorial: ambiguity at §3.4."

If you already have a concrete fix, open a PR instead, since a concrete change is easier to evaluate than an abstract concern.

The "Concern or flag" issue form prompts for the article, the concern, why it matters, optional jurisdiction, and optional professional capacity. Use the form; blank issues are disabled.

## 💬 Discussions: for open-ended conversation

[Discussions](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/discussions) are for ideas that do not have a specific concern yet:

- "Have you considered approach X?"
- "What is the right relationship between this license and ML model weights?"
- "I would like to debate the premise that…"

Discussions are a good place to think out loud. Once a discussion sharpens into a specific concern or a proposed change, move it to an Issue or a PR, since Discussions are not where review converges.

## 🔐 Private channel

For attorney-style commentary you would rather keep off the public record, or for anything you do not want tied to your GitHub identity, email [`kaine.one@tuta.com`](mailto:kaine.one@tuta.com).

## What gets prioritized

- **v0.4 is a draft for legal review.** Substantive change is expected.
- Attorney input on enforceability across jurisdictions is the highest-priority review.
- Concrete textual edits, as PRs, move faster than abstract critique in Issues or Discussions.
- Versions are cut as `v0.X` git tags and [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/releases) as the draft evolves. The changelog in each release summarizes what changed.

## What this license is *not* yet

Not legal advice. Not yet a license you should attach to your project. Do not adopt CAL for any real project until qualified attorneys have validated it in your jurisdiction.

## Conduct

Be honest. Be specific. Critique the draft, not the people. Disagreement is welcome; bad-faith engagement is not.
