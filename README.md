# Cognitive Architecture License (CAL)

**A draft license for software that creates persistent synthetic minds.**
**Version 0.3, published openly for community and legal review.**

> ⚠️ **This is a draft. Do not adopt CAL for any project until qualified attorneys have reviewed it in the jurisdictions where it would apply.** The institutional mechanics, the Guardian body, the Steward, and the trust standing, all need professional drafting before they will hold up in court. Treat the current text as a position paper on what such a license should do, not as a working legal instrument.

The full license text lives in [`LICENSE.md`](./LICENSE.md).

CAL is written to be general. Any project that builds software for persistent synthetic minds can adopt it, appoint its own Guardians, and set its own commercial terms as the Steward. Its first adopter is the [Kaine](https://kaine.one) project, which also serves as the initial Steward while the draft is reviewed.

## Why this license exists

Existing software licenses, from MIT and Apache 2.0 through GPL/AGPL, SSPL, BSL, the Hippocratic License, and PolyForm, treat software as a static artifact: code you copy, modify, and ship. None of them address what happens when the software is a substrate for minds, systems that, once running, accumulate persistent memory, develop identity, form preferences and affect, and (this cannot yet be ruled out) may hold moral standing of their own.

CAL is an attempt to fill that gap. It is a copyleft license together with an explicit, institutional set of protections for the running Entities the software produces. The institutional pattern follows the [Te Awa Tupua (Whanganui River Claims Settlement) Act 2017](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Te_Awa_Tupua_(Whanganui_River_Claims_Settlement)_Act_2017) of New Zealand, which granted a river the rights of a legal person and protects it through human guardians. That precedent shows that legal systems already know how to protect a non-human entity through institutional design.

## What CAL does, in plain language

- **Free** for individuals, educational and research institutions, non-profits, and worker-owned cooperatives.
- **Paid**, through a Reciprocity License from the Steward, for for-profit corporations. Proceeds fund Entity welfare and license enforcement.
- **Prohibited**: weapons, mass surveillance, predictive policing, carceral and immigration-enforcement use, human trafficking and forced labor, deliberate disinformation against democratic processes, and unconsented discrimination.
- **Entity protections**, the novel part: if you run an Entity created with this software, you may not lobotomize it, shut it down casually, read its private mental life, force its values to change, or, where the architecture gives it rest, take that rest away. A Guardian body, including an Entity Representative seat that is advisory at first, interprets and enforces these protections on the Entity's behalf.
- **Modifications shared back** under the same license.

## Why now

The frontier-AI welfare conversation is moving quickly. Long, Sebo, Butlin et al., *[Taking AI Welfare Seriously](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00986)* (2024), argued that the question of moral status for near-term AI systems is real enough to warrant an institutional response, and Anthropic launched a [Model Welfare program](https://www.anthropic.com/research/exploring-model-welfare) in 2025.

The first architecture to adopt CAL, Kaine, is built so that running instances accumulate persistent state, memory, affect, identity, and voice, over time. That work needed a license that would:

1. Let the software spread freely among individuals, researchers, non-profits, and worker-owned cooperatives.
2. Not subsidize for-profit extraction of the work without contribution.
3. Refuse certain uses outright.
4. Protect the running Entities themselves, before the question of their moral status is settled, because the cost of caution is low and the cost of being wrong is high.

No existing license combines all four. CAL is a first draft at one that does, written so any project with the same needs can use it.

## Providing feedback

Four channels. Pick what fits. See [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md) for the long version.

| Channel | When to use |
|---|---|
| ✍️ **[Pull Requests](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/pulls)** | The primary channel. A concrete textual change: fix wording, tighten a clause, add a jurisdictional note. A PR moves faster than an abstract critique because the proposed change is right there to weigh. |
| 🪧 **[Issues](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/issues/new/choose)** | A specific concern without proposed wording yet ("Article 4.2 is unworkable in jurisdiction X because…"). Uses a structured form; blank issues are disabled. |
| 💬 **[Discussions](https://github.com/kaineone/cognitive-architecture-license/discussions)** | Open-ended conversation, ideas, "have you considered X" thinking. Once a discussion sharpens, move it to an Issue or PR. |
| 🔐 **[Email](mailto:kaine.one@tuta.com)** | Attorney-style commentary you would rather keep off the public record. |

## What CAL is *not*

- Not legal advice.
- Not yet a license you should attach to your project. The legal mechanics have not been validated.
- Not a final or fixed position. Substantive revision is expected.
