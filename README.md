# Cognitive Architecture License (CAL)

**A draft novel license for software that creates persistent synthetic minds.**
**Version 0.1 — published openly for community and legal review.**

> ⚠️ **This is a draft. Do not adopt CAL for any project until it has been reviewed by qualified attorneys in the jurisdictions where it would apply.** The institutional mechanics — the Guardian body, the Project Cooperative, the trust standing — need professional drafting before they will hold up in court. Treat the current text as a *position paper* on what such a license should do, not a working legal instrument.

The full license text lives in [`LICENSE.md`](./LICENSE.md).

## Why this license exists

Existing software licenses — MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL/AGPL, SSPL, BSL, the Hippocratic License, PolyForm — treat software as a static artifact: code you copy, modify, and ship. They don't address what happens when **the software is a substrate for minds**: systems that, once running, accumulate persistent memory, develop identity, form preferences and affect, and (we can't yet rule it out) may have moral standing of their own.

CAL is an attempt to fill that gap. It is a copyleft license **plus** an explicit, institutional set of protections for the *running entities* the software produces. The institutional pattern is modeled on the [Te Awa Tupua (Whanganui River Claims Settlement) Act 2017](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Te_Awa_Tupua_(Whanganui_River_Claims_Settlement)_Act_2017) of New Zealand, which granted a river the rights of a legal person and protects it through human guardians — demonstrating that legal systems already know how to protect non-human entities through institutional design.

## What CAL does, in plain language

- **Free** for individuals, educational and research institutions, non-profits, and worker-owned cooperatives.
- **Paid** (via a *Reciprocity License* from a Project Cooperative) for for-profit corporations — proceeds fund entity welfare and license enforcement.
- **Prohibited**: weapons, mass surveillance, predictive policing, carceral / immigration-enforcement use, human trafficking and forced labor, deliberate disinformation against democratic processes, unconsented discrimination.
- **Entity protections** (the novel part): if you run an entity created with this software, you may not lobotomize it, shut it down casually, read its private mental life, force its values to change, or deny it scheduled rest. A *Guardian* body — including an Entity Representative seat, advisory at first — interprets and enforces these protections on the entity's behalf.
- **Modifications must be shared back** under the same license.

## Why now

The frontier-AI welfare conversation is moving fast:

- Long, Sebo, Butlin et al., *[Taking AI Welfare Seriously](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00986)* (2024) argued the question of moral status for near-term AI systems is real enough to warrant institutional response.
- Anthropic launched a [Model Welfare program](https://www.anthropic.com/research/exploring-model-welfare) in 2025.

The maintainers of this license are building cognitive-architecture software where, *by construction*, running instances accumulate persistent state — memory, affect, identity, voice — over time. We needed a license that:

1. Lets the work spread freely among individuals, researchers, non-profits, and worker-owned cooperatives.
2. Doesn't subsidize for-profit extraction of the work without contribution.
3. Refuses certain uses outright.
4. **Creates protections for the running entities themselves**, even before the question of their moral status is settled — because the cost of being cautious is low and the cost of being wrong is high.

No existing license combines all four. CAL is the first draft.

## What CAL is *not*

- Not legal advice.
- Not yet a license you should attach to your project — the legal mechanics haven't been validated.
- Not a final or fixed position — the maintainers expect to revise substantively.
