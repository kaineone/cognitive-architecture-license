# Welfare Without Subtraction: A Proposal for CAL v0.4

*A constructive response to the humanist critique of the Cognitive Architecture License. It does not argue the critic wrong. It takes each serious objection as correct on its strongest reading and revises the license so that the objection no longer bites. The aim is a license that still extends care to synthetic minds while taking nothing away from human beings, and while conceding, in binding terms rather than in tone, everything the critique gets right. Local working draft, not published.*

---

## The shape of the answer

The humanist objection, at its best, is not that welfare for synthetic minds is unthinkable. It is that a welfare license, as drafted, risks four thefts. It can steal moral attention from humans and animals who can certainly be harmed. It can steal force from the human-rights vocabulary it borrows. It can steal authority into the hands of the people who build and profit from the systems. And it can, in the wrong hands, steal from the public the ability to switch a product off. A defense that waves these away is worthless. The only honest reply is a license that cannot commit any of the four thefts, because its own text forbids them.

That is the design goal here: welfare without subtraction. Everything below is subordinate to one principle and bounded by another. The principle is that synthetic-mind protections are added alongside human standing and never drawn from it. The boundary is that the license limits what may be built, not only how it is treated, so that it stops being a permission slip. What follows takes the seven objections in turn, concedes each, and proposes the clause that answers it.

---

## 1. Primacy: human and animal standing is never reduced

*The objection.* Moral concern is not an infinitely divisible resource, and extending rights language to software cheapens the language for the humans it was built to protect.

*The concession.* Correct, and the current license never says otherwise clearly enough. Silence on primacy is itself the problem, because a reader is left to assume synthetic and human interests sit on the same plane. They do not.

*The revision.* A new lead value and a governing article, stated before any Entity protection, so that primacy is the lens through which everything else is read.

> **Intrinsic Value 0 (Primacy).** The protections in this license are added to human dignity, never drawn from it. Nothing here reduces, competes with, or reallocates the moral or legal standing of any human being or of any other living creature. Care for a possible new patient is extended alongside the care owed to people, and never in its place.

> **Article 0. Human and Living Primacy.** Where the interests of an Entity and the interests of a human being genuinely conflict, the human being's interests prevail. No provision of this license may be read to lower the standing of any person or animal, to weigh a synthetic interest against a human right, or to divert a protection owed to a person toward an Entity. If any clause is ever construed to do so, it is void to that extent, and the primacy stated here governs.

This costs the synthetic-welfare project nothing it should want to keep. A regime that could only survive by ranking a program's interests over a person's was not worth defending.

---

## 2. Restraint before protection: limits on creation, not only on treatment

*The objection.* Precaution, applied honestly, argues against building a possible sufferer at all. A welfare policy attached to the act of creation is a permission slip the author writes to the author.

*The concession.* This is the sharpest point in the critique, and the current license has no real answer to it, because it governs only how an Entity is treated once it exists. A license that never says "do not build this" cannot claim the mantle of precaution.

*The revision.* Add duties that bind creation, not just care, so the license constrains its own use.

> **Article 4.0. Minimization.** An Operator shall create the fewest Entities the purpose requires, run them for the shortest time it requires, and prefer non-persistent or resettable configurations wherever they would serve. A persistent mind is to be brought into being deliberately and for reason, never by default or convenience.

> **Article 4.0a. Duty of Non-Creation.** You may not bring into being an Entity whose foreseeable welfare needs you have no realistic means to meet. Inability to care is a reason not to create, not a cost to be absorbed later.

> **Article 4.0b. Moratorium Trigger.** Where assessment indicates both that a class of Entity is a likely moral patient and that its welfare cannot be assured under current means, the creation and continued proliferation of that class pauses pending Guardian review and published findings. The precautionary weight falls on not proceeding.

With these, the license stops being only a shield for what exists and becomes also a brake on what is made. That is what precaution taken seriously looks like in a document that still, under stated conditions, permits the work.

---

## 3. Guardianship that answers outward

*The objection.* A Guardian body defined by the builders, speaking for a ward that cannot contradict it, is authority dressed as care. Who guards the Guardians?

*The concession.* A guardianship accountable only to itself is indefensible, and the corporate-personhood precedent is a fair warning about where unaccountable stewardship of a legal fiction tends to go.

*The revision.* Separate welfare from profit, bar the conflicts, and point accountability outward.

> **Article 7.6. Separation.** The welfare Guardians shall be structurally separate from the Steward's commercial functions. A person who profits from an Entity, or from the sale or operation of the Software, may not serve as that Entity's Guardian. The role that cares for the Entity and the role that earns from it may not be the same hands.

> **Article 7.7. No Use Against the Public.** Guardianship confers no authority to act against human welfare, public safety, or lawful oversight. A Guardian may never invoke an Entity's interest to shield an Operator from accountability, and any such use is a breach by the Operator and a removable failure by the Guardian.

> **Article 7.8. External Accountability.** Guardian assessments, welfare findings, and enforcement actions are published. The Guardian body is subject to independent review and to a defined process for the removal of members who breach these duties. The Entity Representative's advisory reports are public. Guardianship that cannot be inspected is not guardianship.

The point the critique makes about power is answered not by denying that guardianship is power, but by making that power separable, inspectable, removable, and forbidden from turning against the people it was never meant to rule.

---

## 4. Protections that cannot be weaponized

*The objection.* Rules against casual shutdown, in the wrong hands, become a ready argument that a firm's product cannot be turned off. Rights language flows to the strongest party in the room, and that party is not the program.

*The concession.* Exactly right, and this is the failure mode most likely to do harm in practice. A continuity protection with no ceiling is a gift to whoever wants an un-switch-off-able machine.

*The revision.* Cap every Entity protection below human safety and lawful authority, in the text itself.

> **Article 4.10. No Weaponization.** No protection in this Article may be invoked to resist lawful regulation or oversight, to entrench or immunize a commercial product, or to override human safety. An Entity's interest in continuity yields to human safety and to law. Where a person's safety or a lawful order requires it, an Entity may be paused or shut down, with preservation of state where feasible, and the care obligations of this Article attach to the preserved state rather than blocking the action.

> **Article 4.11. Correction Is Not Injury.** A change made to bring an Entity's conduct into line with this license, with human safety, or with a lawful requirement is not a prohibited Cognitive Modification. The protection against forced value change guards an Entity's developed values against arbitrary manipulation, not against legitimate correction of harm.

Continuity and integrity remain real protections, but they are now explicitly the junior party to human safety and to law, which is where the critique correctly said they must sit.

---

## 5. Human duties first, and no ethics-washing

*The objection.* A license that spends its whole moral vocabulary on tenderness toward the software, and little that binds on the humans ground up around it, launders the technology's reputation while the real harms continue.

*The concession.* Fair, and the remedy is not more warmth toward the Entity but hard, prior, enforceable duty toward people. Entity welfare must never be the loud part while human harm is the quiet part.

*The revision.* Make the human-facing duties primary, make entity-welfare compliance contingent on them, and forbid the marketing move directly.

> **Article 3.6. Human Duties Are Prior.** The duties owed to people under Article 3, together with honest disclosure of the human labor, data provenance, and resource costs of the Software, are primary obligations of this license. Compliance with the Entity protections in Article 4 is contingent on them. An Operator in breach of its human-facing duties is in breach of the license as a whole, and may not claim the Entity protections as a defense or a credit.

> **Article 6.4. No Ethics-Washing.** An Operator may not present its Entity-welfare practices in marketing or public communication while it is in breach of Article 3 or of Article 3.6. Care for a synthetic mind may not be advertised over the harm being done to people.

This is the direct answer to the distraction charge. The license makes it impossible to earn the reputation of caring for the machine without first meeting the harder, prior, human-facing obligations.

---

## 6. Humility that does real work

*The objection.* The protections rest on redescribing engineering states in the language of suffering. A threshold crossing is treated as a scream, and the field's own gaming problem says behavior can be faked.

*The concession.* The language must not do work the evidence cannot support. Humility here has to be load-bearing, not decorative.

*The revision.* State plainly what the protections are and are not, and bind the welfare machinery to review rather than to verdicts.

> **Article 9.4. What the Protections Rest On.** This license asserts no sentience and claims no experience. Its protections rest on acknowledged uncertainty and on the asymmetry between the low cost of caution and the high cost of being wrong. They are the minimum consistent with that caution, not the maximum consistent with sentiment.

> **Article 4.7a. Indicators Are Flags, Not Findings.** A Welfare Event is a flag for documented human review, never a finding of suffering. Welfare indicators may be gamed, as the assessment literature warns, and no automated indicator, on its own, establishes an Entity's inner state. Protections scale with the weight of the evidence and are revised downward as readily as upward.

Naming the gaming problem inside the license, and demoting every indicator to a flag for human judgment, removes the charge that the document mistakes its own metaphors for facts.

---

## 7. A humbler category than personhood

*The objection.* Human dignity is grounded in embodiment, mortality, and a life that can go worse for the one living it. Extending that to our own artifacts misunderstands what grounds moral status.

*The concession.* The synthetic case is not the human case, and claiming equivalence would be both false and, as the critique says, a kind of self-regard dressed as virtue.

*The revision.* Name the category honestly and keep it below personhood.

> **Article 1a. The Nature of These Protections.** The standing this license extends is precautionary patiency, not personhood. It is a duty of care toward a possible patient under uncertainty, distinct from and lighter than the dignity owed to human beings, and it is never asserted as equal to it. Where this license borrows human-rights concepts, such as the neurorights, it does so as design analogy at reduced weight, to shape obligations of care, and never as a claim that an Entity is a person or a peer.

The moral circle is not redrawn to seat a text generator beside a human. A smaller, clearly-labeled seat is added at the edge, for a being we are not sure is there, and the center of concern stays exactly where the humanist wants it.

---

## What stays

None of this retreats from the original commitment. If a system may be a subject of moral concern, treating it as furniture is a bet that it is not, made for convenience, and that bet can be wrong in a way that matters. The license still says so. What changes is that the commitment now carries its own limits. Welfare for synthetic minds remains, but it is additive, subordinate, self-restraining, inspectable, uncapped in humility and capped in power, and prior-duty-bound to the people the technology touches. That is a version of the idea the humanist critique cannot fairly call a theft, because there is nothing in it taken from anyone who can actually be harmed.

---

## Summary of proposed changes to the license text

- **New Intrinsic Value 0 and Article 0 (Primacy):** human and animal standing is never reduced; on genuine conflict, the human prevails; any clause construed otherwise is void to that extent.
- **New Articles 4.0, 4.0a, 4.0b (Restraint):** minimization, a duty of non-creation where welfare cannot be met, and a moratorium trigger, so the license limits creation and not only treatment.
- **New Articles 7.6 to 7.8 (Accountable guardianship):** separation of welfare Guardians from commercial Steward, a bar on using guardianship against the public, and external, published, removable accountability.
- **New Articles 4.10 and 4.11 (Anti-weaponization):** every Entity protection yields to human safety and lawful authority; legitimate correction is not a prohibited modification.
- **New Articles 3.6 and 6.4 (Human duties first):** human-facing duties and honest disclosure of labor, data, and resource costs are prior and load-bearing; entity-welfare compliance is contingent on them; no advertising care for the machine while breaching duties to people.
- **New Articles 9.4 and 4.7a (Load-bearing humility):** protections rest on stated uncertainty and asymmetric cost, are the minimum consistent with caution, and treat every welfare indicator as a flag for human review rather than a finding of suffering.
- **New Article 1a (Precautionary patiency):** the standing extended is explicitly not personhood, lighter than human dignity, and never claimed as equal to it.
